EdgeMD

A portable English–Spanish medical translator that trains on the cloud and runs inference on the edge — enabling real-time bilingual communication between doctors and patients, anywhere.

In Progress Deadline: April 24, 2025 Repo: upenn-embedded/final-project-edge-md
About

Project Overview

Many patients in the US and in underserved communities abroad rely on a human interpreter to communicate with their doctor. Relying on an intermediary raises HIPAA concerns and limits how many patients a physician can serve. EdgeMD removes that bottleneck — a compact, low-cost device that any NGO, clinic, or field physician can carry and use without network connectivity at the point of care.

Architecture

System Design

On a button press the device records audio through an I2S MEMS microphone, runs it through a pretrained speech-to-text and translation model on the Raspberry Pi 5, then plays back the translated result through an I2S amplifier and speaker. The STM32 handles GPIO — buttons and LED state indicators — and communicates with the Pi over UART.

Requirements

Software Requirements (SRS)

The pipeline covers three stages: speech-to-text, translation, and post-processing. A pretrained model (Whisper or Hugging Face) runs on the edge device; a medical dictionary enforces terminology accuracy.

ID Requirement
SRS-01 Convert spoken English or Spanish to text with ≥ 90% accuracy under low-noise conditions, measured against ground-truth transcriptions.
SRS-02 Translate between English and Spanish with ≥ 85% semantic accuracy on a predefined set of medical phrases.
SRS-03 Apply a medical dictionary so critical terms are translated correctly in ≥ 95% of test cases.
SRS-04 Produce translated audio output within 15 seconds of input completion, measured end-to-end.
SRS-05 Preserve conversational meaning across at least two consecutive sentences.
SRS-06 Detect low-confidence or failed translations and trigger an error/retry in ≥ 90% of such cases.

Hardware Requirements (HRS)

The hardware provides a full audio I/O pipeline with tactile controls and clear visual state feedback via LEDs.

ID Requirement
HRS-01 LEDs shall indicate system state: power on, active listening, processing, and error/low-confidence.
HRS-02 The audio input path shall capture intelligible speech in minimal-noise environments suitable for clinical use.
HRS-03 The system shall operate in real-time or near-real-time — speech to translated playback with minimal perceptible delay.
HRS-04 Tactile push buttons shall control the pipeline (record, mode switch) via GPIO to the STM32.
HRS-05 The audio output path shall drive a 4 Ω speaker through the I2S Class-D amplifier for intelligible playback.
